Hwiyo Hwiyo Mobile Application

  • Problem Busy workers and commuters lack the time necessary to walk or travel to buy fruits and vegetables
  • Goal Design an app for Hwiyo Hwiyo that enables users to easily order and have delivered fresh fruits and vegetables.

  • Research

    To get a better understanding of the consumers I'm creating for and their requirements, I conducted interviews and built empathy maps. Working adults who don't have time to visit the market for fruit and vegetables were identified as a main user category during research. Although this user group confirmed previous predictions about Hwiyo Hwiyo customers, study also found that time was not the only factor preventing users from cooking at home. Obligations, interests, or challenges that make it difficult to get groceries or go to restaurants in person were among the other user issues.

    PERSONAS

  • Ideation

    I did a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas for how to address gaps identified in the competitive audit.

  • Initial

    After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the Utility Bills purchasing app. These designs focused on helping the user purchase their utility bills .

  • Key insights

    I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.
    1. Users want to order fruits and vegetables quickly
    2. Users want to see the order composition and delivery information on submitting order.
    3. The checkout process has too many unnecessary steps
    4. The process of adding products to your order is a bit confusing

  • Solution

    Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design changes like providing a transactions filtering pane that allows users to filter their transactions base on their need rather than just plain listing.
